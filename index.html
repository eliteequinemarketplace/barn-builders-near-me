What to Look for in a Horse Barn Builder

The most important qualities in a horse barn builder are specific experience with equestrian facilities, familiarity with the ventilation, drainage, and safety requirements unique to horse barns, and a portfolio of completed equestrian projects you can visit and evaluate. Key elements of quality horse barn construction include adequate stall size (14x14 minimum for warmbloods), proper ventilation to prevent respiratory issues, safe materials throughout (no exposed sharp edges, appropriate flooring), adequate drainage in wash stalls and aisles, and a well-designed tack room and feed storage area.

Indoor Arena Construction for Hunter Jumper

An indoor riding arena is one of the most significant investments a hunter jumper facility can make — and the quality of the footing, drainage, and structural design determines how useful and durable the arena will be for decades. Hunter jumper indoor arenas need sufficient ceiling height for jumping (minimum 16 feet at the center, 20+ feet preferred), quality all-weather footing, proper drainage beneath the surface, adequate lighting for evening rides and show preparation, and ideally viewing areas for trainers and spectators.
